## 📊 How the Free Presets Work

Lightroom presets are `.xmp` files containing a snapshot of development settings. When you apply a preset, Lightroom reads these settings and maps them to the current photo. For instance:

- **Exposure & Contrast** – balanced for both RAW and JPEG.
- **Tone Curve** – custom S‑curve for filmic contrast or flat for a matte look.
- **HSL / Color Grading** – precise hue shifts (e.g., greens to teal in travel presets).
- **Grain & Vignette** – subtle grain for analog feel, edge darkening for focus.

> Because the presets are non‑destructive, you can stack them or use them as a starting point for your own style – all at zero cost.

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Presets don’t show up after import.  
**Solution:** Restart Lightroom. If they still don’t appear, ensure you imported `.xmp` files, not `.lrtemplate` (old format). Check the correct preset location under Develop.

**Problem:** Preset looks different on my photo.  
**Solution:** Presets are designed for a wide range, but lighting conditions can vary. Adjust the **White Balance** and **Exposure** first – often that’s all that’s needed.

**Problem:** I’m on Lightroom Mobile, can’t import directly.  
**Solution:** Import the `.xmp` files into Lightroom CC desktop (Mac or Windows) and they will sync automatically to your mobile device via Creative Cloud.

**Problem:** ZIP file won’t unzip.  
**Solution:** Use the built‑in Archive Utility on Mac (just double‑click). If issues persist, try a free tool like The Unarchiver.
